Frequently Asked Questions

What is the right age for Pre-School?

The right age to start preschool is typically between 2 to 3 years, as children are more socially, emotionally, and physically ready to adapt to a structured learning environment.

What is the curriculum followed at APJ?

The curriculum at APJ is a blend of the Finland-based and American systems. The Finland curriculum emphasizes play-based, child-led learning with a focus on creativity, while the American curriculum balances structured academics and play, focusing on foundational skills like literacy and numeracy through interactive methods.

What are the special features of APJ International?

APJ International Preschool offers a nurturing environment with a 6:1 child-to-caregiver ratio, very kind caretakers, a globally inspired curriculum, and an inviting environment that combines academic excellence and holistic development.

How are safety measures handled at APJ International Preschool?

APJ International Preschool prioritizes child safety with secure premises, constant supervision, and CCTV monitoring. Regular safety drills, childproof facilities, and trained staff ensure a safe and nurturing environment for every child.

To what extent do preschools expect parents to be involved in their child’s education and activities?

Preschools vary in their expectations for parental involvement, but greater parental involvement generally supports a child's development and well-being. Active participation in your child's life is highly beneficial.

What educational philosophy is commonly followed in preschools?

Preschools often focus on academic methods to prepare children for primary school while fostering harmony with nature and encouraging children to learn through play and exploration.

What is the benefit of a child going to a preschool?

Preschool plays a vital role in your child's social and emotional growth, teaching them to collaborate, respect others, and tackle challenges. It offers a supportive space where children can explore their interests and develop foundational skills for later academic success.
